Story

Our objective is to bridge the gap between all National Parks throughout the United States with a unique overlay of historical fire data that may influence your next camping decision.

Wildfires Causation

Wildfires are normally unplanned and unwanted events that are triggered either by lightning-caused fires, unauthorized human-caused fires, and escaped prescribed fire projects. Fun Fact: 90% of all wildfires are started by humans. According to the Congressional Research service, "The Forest Service within the U.S. Department of Agriculture carries out wildfire management and response across the 193 million acres of the National Forest System (NFS)." Whereas the Department of the Interior better known as the DOI manages most the States wildfire response for more than 400 million acrees of national. parks, wildlife refuges and pereseves historical public lands and Indian reservations. In 2020, over 58K wildfires took place and burned 10.3 million acres the most acreage impacted in a year. Nearly 40% of these acres took place in California.

National Parks

In 1916, President Woodrow Wilson implemented an act around the National Park Service. This service protects the sceneries, historic objects and the wild life environment. There is total 407 sites with 28 different designations that is protected under this act. These include 127 historical parks, 78 national monuments, 59 national parks, 25 battlefields, 19 preserves, 18 recreations areas, 10 seashores, four parkways, four lakesores, and two reserves (Sited by Outdoor Project).
